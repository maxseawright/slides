Em que sentido se usa a palavra “contexto” no “Relatório anual consolidado 2017” do Itaú Unibanco?

Famosamente Robert Silvers (1929–2017), fundador e co-editor de *The New York Review of Books*, odiava o uso comum de “context.” Numa entrevista de 2013 comentou o seguinte:

The most heretical thing we do is try to avoid *context*. *Context* has an original, useful meaning, now generally lost: the actual language surrounding a particular text—*con*, meaning “with,” and text—and now it’s used for every set of surrounding circumstances or state of things, and it gets worse with *contextualize*, *sometimes* used to mean some sort of justification.
Original emphasis. Danner, Mark. “In Conversation: Robert Silvers”. *New York*. [online](http://nymag.com/news/features/robert-silvers-2013-4/).
