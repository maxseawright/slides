synecdoche

A trope or figure of speech involving the substitution of part for whole, genus for species, or vice versa. For instance, ‘get your butt over here!’ (presumably accompanied by the rest of their body). Some theorists do not distinguish synecdoche from metonymy.

Basic elements of Ashes of the Amazon

**Letters** Letters from Ranulfo (Ran) to Raimundo (Mundo) One letter from Mundo to Lavo (in the novel’s opening) **Relationships** Tense relationship between Raimundo (Mundo) and his supposed father Trajano Mattoso (Jano) Relationships in the orbit of Mundo’s and Jano’s

Lavo reads the letter Mundo wrote before he died. Lavo decides to tell the story that starts some 20 years earlier. Some of the information is unknown to our narrator. Telling the story relies on the letters from Ranulfo to Mundo, as well as the impressions and memories of Ran Naiá’s (who lives with the Mattoso family).
