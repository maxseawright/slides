Simón Bolívar was born into an elite criollo family. In this usage, criollo (sometimes ‘creole’) means people born in the Americas of full or nearly full European heritage. Simón Bolívar’s aristocratic upbringing and mixed record as a military leader lead some, including Karl Marx, to dismiss him as a revolutionary figure.

Karl Marx, “Bolivar y Ponte,” First published in the *New American Cyclopedia*, Vol. III, 1858. [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1858/01/bolivar.htm).
This differs significantly from other usages of the word creole, which often explicitly refers to people of mixed racial heritage, or linguistically, a stable language grown from a mixture of multiple languages.

“The diversity of origins requires an infinitely firm and infinitely delicate touch to manage this heterogeneous society whose complex structure becomes dislocated, divided, and dissolved at the slightest alteration.”

Brazilian Independence & Republic
