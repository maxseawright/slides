from “Poetry, opression & censorship in Latin America”

“In ‘Cálice’, composed jointly with Gilberto Gil, he addresses the subject directly through a pun between ‘cálice’ (chalice or cup) and ‘cale-se’ (shut up), in the biblical phrase ‘father, take this calice from me’. The censors were not deceived however and prohibited the song from being played even without the words. Buarque’s work is remarkable for seeking in colloquial speech a means of reflecting extreme forms of humiliation and abuse (‘Say yes and you'll get on in life’).”

from “Poetry, opression & censorship in Latin America”

“Geraldo Vandré was tortured so brutally that he could no longer write or perform. His ‘Caminhando’, hugely successfully before it was banned, became a kind of touchstone and anthem for all those resisting the dictators. Caetano Veloso and Gilberto Gil ended up as exiles in London, where the former wrote and performed several songs in English, among them ‘London, London’ and ‘It’s a long way’.”
