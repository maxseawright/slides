### Visual culture Week three, day two BUS 3940-002 | University of Utah
The International Program of the Museum of Modern Art was founded in 1952 and supported by the Museum’s International Arts Council, a group of art patrons and community leaders who wished “to let it be known especially in Europe that America was not the cultural backwater that the Russians were trying to demonstrate that it was.”19
An initial five-year grant of $625,000 for the council came primarily from the Rockefeller Brothers Fund, and was used to pay for transport, publicity, and publications related to exhibitions of American art in Europe.20 Among the thirty-three international exhibitions sponsored by MOMA between 1950 and 1956 were “Twelve Contemporary American Painters and Sculptors” (1953–54), “Young Painters” (1956), “Modern Art in the U.S.” (1956), and “The New American Painting” (1958).
